Object Oriented Programming

Object Oriented Programming, otherwise known as OOP, is a method of modeling things we want our programs to represent visually, and/or providing easy access to features that would otherwise require complex methods. For example, you can use an object to make a list of students and all their details such as name, age, grade, class, instructor, or any other information you might need. Objects are collections of information and/or functionality (often containing functions and variables.) The variables are referred to as properties and the functions are called methods.
