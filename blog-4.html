CS:GO Strategy Guide

Choosing a Weapon

There are a wide variety of weapons to be found in Counter Strike: Global Offensive. A number of them are tempting for beginners to start with since they look pretty impressive. As an example, beginners may be disappointed if they start with the Desert Eagle or AWP at they will find it hard to get a good head shot out of them. The best course of action here is to choose a weapon is more effective as represented in the table below:

Use these weapons until you are at a sufficient skill level to move on to others. This will bring more wins in your game sessions and make you a better player overall.

Improving Accuracy

It's best learn how to use your weapons more effectively and there are a few aspects to learn about such as weapon damage, spray pattern and rate of fire. When approching an enemy be sure not to just empty your magazine on them, it's far better use burst fire (3 to 6 shots at a time) or a single accurate headshot, otherwise known as tapping. Below are lists of information that shows the benefits of tapping and burst fire.

Tapping

This technique is typically used in long range situations. The shots are to be made standing still, one shot at a time, and strafing to cover immediatly afterwards so you have a break for the recoil. Advantages of this technique are:

  • Minimal Recoil for more accuracy
  • Moving around is easier
  • Keeps reloading to a minimum

Burst Fire

Burst fire is best used in close range and possibly medium range. Here you will need to fire 3-6 shots at a time and as with tapping you need to stand still while shooting and take cover after. You will find the following advantages with this technique:

  • Shots are more accurate
  • Can be more effective in taking out opponents
  • Expend less ammunition

These are just a few techniques to get you started. There's much more to explore in CS:GO and it would be advised to do further research on the many topics of this game.
