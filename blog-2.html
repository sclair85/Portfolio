Password Management

Password management is very important to implement with various accounts most people may have on the internet. It can help keep your private information safe from those with malicious intent. Surprisingly the most common passwords are the most simplistic type of passwords you could think of. For example, “123456’, ‘qwerty’, ‘77777777’, ‘1111111’, etc. Passwords like these should never be used as they could be hacked very quickly. To help avoid this there are a few key things we need to do:

  • Make sure your password isn’t the same as your user name
  • Use little to no repeating characters
  • Use little to no consecutive characters (such as ‘1234’ or ‘abcd’)
  • Keep password length over 10 characters long
  • Do further research on good password practices

Another common issue is recycling passwords. More and more users open online accounts, creating too many passwords for them to remember, which eventually leads to the dangerous habit of recycling passwords. Thousands of compromised login credentials can be tested in just seconds against many different shopping sites, social medial accounts, and online banks by hacking their software. Be sure to never reuse your passwords for important accounts. A good way to keep all your passwords organized while not losing track of which one goes where is to use a program called a “password manager”. In addition to serving as a sort of extra memory, a password manager will save you time by allowing you to enter your passwords across several devices. A few companies that offer this service are Dashlane, 1Password and Lastpass. Be sure to research each company further to see which is right for you or if there are and additional companies out there that offer similar services.

On top of good password practices and password managers there is an extra security measure you can take which is using Two-Factor Authentication otherwise known as 2FA. With 2FA, a user enters both their username and a password to ensure that the person attempting to access an online account is who they claim to be. However, it is not as simple as gaining access right away, they must provide another factor as well which may include:

  • A key stroke pattern
  • A PIN
  • Answering secret questions
  • A Password
  • Credit card number
  • Finger print
  • Iris scan
  • Voice print

With this security measure it is not likely that anyone trying to steal your account information will have the second security measure to do so keeping user accounts safe.
